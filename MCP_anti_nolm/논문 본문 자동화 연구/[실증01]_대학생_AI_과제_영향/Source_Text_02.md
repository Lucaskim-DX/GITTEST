# Research Summary: Interaction with Soft Tissues

### 1. Role of Soft Tissues
Soft tissues including muscles, ligaments, and fascia stabilize the spine.
- **Instability**: Damage to the Anterior Longitudinal Ligament (ALL) can lead to segmental instability even without bone fracture.
- **Myofascial Pain**: Hardening of soft tissues can compress nerves, simulating spinal nerve root pain.

### 2. Correlation
Chronic inflammation in spinal joints (as seen in Spondylitis) leads to calcium deposition in surrounding soft tissues, resulting in rigid immobilization.
MRI studies show that disc herniations often occur alongside ligamentous laxity.
